Who is Gordon Siebert?

I grew up in a row house in Pittsburgh, PA with my parents, sister and grandparents all living in a small space. My parents slept in the dining room so my grandparents could have their own room. Depression survivors, they taught me how to live frugally and how to work hard.

After service as a Marine in WWII, my Dad Albert worked most of his career managing a grocery store. My Mom and Grandma stayed at home to run the house. My Mom Blanche was able to volunteer and became President of her City of Hope Chapter. I observed how hard my Dad worked six days a week. This gave me a strong work ethic but also encouraged me to pursue higher education.

After attending public schools through high school, I attended Carnegie-Mellon University, served as a member of the Student Senate, student member of the University Board of Trustees, President of Alpha Phi Omega Service Fraternity, and graduated with BS in Civil Engineering and later with an MS in Public Policy & Management.

While in school, I started and ran a business publishing telephone directories for universities and also managed a taxi company. My public service began as a surveyor for the City of Pittsburgh. After graduate school, I worked for the City of Rockville, MD as a Budget Director and Community Development Manager. When I left, the City gave me an award for having managed the City’s budget as tightly as if I was spending the money from my own wallet.

My private employer moved me to California in 1980, where I rose to Director of Public Works in Rancho Palos Verdes and then became City Manager in Palos Verdes Estates. There I learned to manage staff, balance budgets, deliver quality services and work with Council.

My family moved to Morgan Hill in 1989, where I served as Director of Public Works until 1995. Among my accomplishments in Morgan Hill are building the first phase of Butterfield (then Sutter) Blvd., rebuilding the City’s fire hydrants, and building the waste water treatment plant in conjunction with the City of Gilroy. I was also simultaneously Interim Finance Director from 1990 to 1991, where I worked with the City Manager to balance the budget, eliminating a projected 25% deficit. I subsequently served as Director of Public Works in Daly City and Deputy Director of Public Works in San Jose. Currently, I work for Monterey County. I am a Registered Civil Engineer in California which allowed me to work as a partner in an Engineering Consulting business to help numerous cities solve problems and run more efficiently.

My civic involvement has included an appointment to the Santa Clara County Measure A/B (Transportation Sales Tax) Citizens Watchdog Committee, where I also served as Audit Committee Chairperson, and on the San Mateo County Transportation Technical Advisory Committee. I have been a member of the Morgan Hill Rotary Club, Boy Scouts and served as a Board Member of Congregation Emeth. At the National level, I serve on two committees of the American Public Works Association, Education and Buildings & Grounds. I’ve been married for 29 years to Esther and have three adult children, all of whom attended Morgan Hill public schools.

I like working with my hands in my spare time: gardening, restoring old cars and doing my own home improvements. I also love to read; it's one of my favorite ways to relax. I love living in Morgan Hill. I love the natural beauty, the friendliness of the community and the small town ambience.

That’s why I’m running. To protect our quality of life, continue to improve on our destination downtown, and to bring financial security to our city budget.
