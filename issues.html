Gordon's Positions on Issues

Gordon Siebert on Public Safety Issues in Morgan Hill

It is important to maintain our local police department. Because our police force is integrated into the fabric of our community, the department and its officers have the sensitivity to provide the kind of community policing that gets the best results. However, I’m open to exploring contracting options, especially for specialized services such as a bomb squad or hostage negotiator, if those services can be integrated with our own Police Department.

As a City Manager, I oversaw the Police Department and hired a new Police Chief. It is very important that we choose the right Police Chief for Morgan Hill and I believe that you and I can make a contribution to that process.

Morgan Hill is fortunate to be a safe place to live, with a crime rate below the county’s average. However, there are problems and we must seek opportunities to make the city even safer. We have an outstanding Police Department that is working under difficult financial constraints. Their effectiveness comes from solid leadership, employee training and being provided with the right tools. Efficiency is achieved through smart personnel selection and the best utilization and deployment of resources.

We all can help support our local Police Department by volunteering, supporting the Community Law Enforcement Foundation and participating in the upcoming forums to guide the selection of our next Police Chief.

Fire safety and emergency medical care as well as traffic control are important too. We can be good citizens by eliminating hazards such as overgrown weeds. Emergency preparedness, an area in which Morgan Hill excels, offers opportunities for everyone to be involved, in one’s own home or by participating at the community level. Other important elements to protect public safety include street lighting, traffic safety and the wide availability of recreational activities.

My Public Safety Accomplishments

  • Worked with the Rockville, MD Police Department to secure a grant to hire more police officers
  • Attended team-building exercises with two police chiefs while serving as Palos Verdes Estates’ City Manager
  • Competed for and secured nearly a dozen traffic safety grants in cities throughout California
  • Worked with residents to raise funds to enhance police, fire and emergency medical service
  • Negotiated a contract with Los Angeles County which improved fire services and lowered costs


Gordon Siebert on Economic Development Issues in Morgan Hill

What does economic development mean for you and Morgan Hill?

  • More Jobs for Morgan Hill residents
  • Accessibility to shopping for needed goods and services
  • More revenue for public safety and city services
  • Employers who support civic activities

Economic development and growth control work together to create a city with predictable, steady population increases and business development that creates a balanced city that works. Our outstanding quality of life with its country ambiance, natural beauty, numerous recreational opportunities and attractive housing are promoted by Morgan Hill staff to attract new employers and shopping to Morgan Hill as we also provide assistance to our local businesses which are suffering from this economic downturn. Our local businesses are the lifeblood of Morgan Hill and deserve to be supported by our whole community. It’s great to shop in places where proprietors understand our needs and even know our names. That’s part of what makes Morgan Hill feel special.

The Council needs to articulate its goal of fostering businesses through a variety of efforts, including helpful development processing, connecting businesses with prospective sites for entry and expansion, and reaching out to businesses to help them overcome obstacles to success.

For example, if an auto dealer faces a shortage of trained technicians, the City might make a connection to the Gavillan College automotive technician program. The City might make space available in several of its many facilities for weekly networking meetings. Fostering volunteer efforts to assist in offsetting City service reductions in police, parks and recreation not only improves services but also creates another networking opportunity. These ideas are but a few of the many that we can initiate and pursue in promoting Morgan Hill as a great place to do business and live.

My Economic Development Accomplishments

  • Helped to shepherd development through the City’s review process to allow businesses like Safeway, Long’s Drugs and Maple Leaf RV Park to be built, thereby creating jobs and City revenue
  • Revised the City’s sewer rate to make business fees more equitable
  • Participated in the Residential Development Control System review to maintain quality housing
